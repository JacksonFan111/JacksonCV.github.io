About

I build the evaluation harnesses, memory systems, tool-use scaffolds, and audit trails that let AI systems improve safely and measurably.

Data/BI developer and independent AI research reader with a practical systems background, targeting research-assistant and research-engineering work on recursive self-improvement. My edge is translating ambiguous real-world problems into reliable data pipelines, reproducible evaluations, and executive-readable evidence.

I read frontier papers, extract their mechanisms, and turn them into diagrams, workflows, dashboards, and implementation plans — bridging research and engineering. DGM-style self-improvement only works when proposed changes are tested, ranked, and archived, and my professional work is built around exactly that: reliable metrics, audit evidence, lineage, and stakeholder trust.

I want to help build the systems that let AI improve safely, cheaply, recursively, and measurably.

Power BI · SSAS · RLS · Production analytics

Lightweight IAS reporting model

  • Problem: the full enterprise tabular model was too large, and security-grain evaluation created memory pressure.
  • Solution: a compact import model using monthly aggregation, period anchors, RLS adviser/PWA logic, and controlled dimensional scaffolding.
  • Why it matters for AI research: demonstrates building compute-aware evaluation infrastructure rather than brute-force systems.
CRM audit reconstruction · Operations / analytics

Onboarding timeline evidence system

  • Problem: the business needed a credible timeline from account readiness to open-to-trade status across imperfect CRM evidence.
  • Solution: mapped multiple evidence sources, reconstructed chronological paths, flagged event-order issues, and produced summary/detail outputs for review.
  • Why it matters for AI research: analogous to agent lineage — trace actions, preserve evidence, diagnose sequence errors, and make hidden process dynamics visible.
